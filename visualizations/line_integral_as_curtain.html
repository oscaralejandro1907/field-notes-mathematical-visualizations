Exhibit 04 · Line Integral

Line Integral as a Curtain

A scalar line integral made tangible. A curtain hangs from a curve drawn on the surface z = f(x,y) straight down to the xy-plane — and the area of that curtain is the integral. Drag to rotate, and watch the ribbon sweep out beneath the path.

Interactive 3D visualization of a line integral as the area of a curtain hanging from a curve on a surface down to the xy-plane.

curve C on surface
projection of C on xy-plane
curtain = ∫C f ds

The height of the curtain at any point along the path is the value of the function there, f(x,y). Summed over the whole curve, that sweep of area is exactly the scalar line integral ∫C f ds — the "wall" standing on the curve C with the surface as its skyline.
